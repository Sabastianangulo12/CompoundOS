# Product Roadmap

## Positioning

The Compound Lifting Club OS should not try to become a generic gym CRM.

The strongest position for this product is:

- stronger operator backbone than an internal spreadsheet stack
- more culture and member identity than Gymdesk
- more gym-specific daily utility than Glofox
- one system for owners, staff, front desk, and members

In short:

- Gymdesk = practical admin software
- Glofox = polished commercial fitness platform
- Compound OS = premium gym operating system with a real member app

## What We Keep

These are already differentiators and should be protected as we grow:

- member app with real lifestyle utility, not just billing access
- highlights, PRs, visits, steps, and gym news
- community and friend-based activity
- training, workouts, and coach direction living close together
- front desk payment QR concept
- fridge, merch, and in-gym commerce direction
- owner dashboard that already thinks in terms of operations, not just pages

## What We Must Improve

These are the areas where Gymdesk and Glofox still have the advantage:

- billing maturity
- membership plan administration
- subscription lifecycle visibility
- front desk reliability
- reporting and audit trails
- staff permissions and role guardrails
- production-grade polish around operational workflows

## Product Principles

1. Every major feature should help one of three jobs:
   - run the gym
   - retain the member
   - deepen the gym culture

2. The dashboard should feel like an operating system, not a collection of forms.

3. The member app should feel like a premium gym companion, not a watered-down admin portal.

4. Features that create daily engagement are more valuable than features that only look good in a feature checklist.

5. We only build class-studio patterns when they fit the actual gym model.

## Phase 1: Must-Have To Compete

Goal: become trustworthy enough to replace patchwork tools.

### Dashboard

- memberships and billing workspace
- plan creation and editing
- member subscription assignment
- card/payment status visibility
- freeze, resume, cancel lifecycle timeline
- front desk command center
- member detail page with notes, visits, billing state, and status history
- basic reporting for active members, freezes, cancels, attendance, and revenue
- role-based staff permissions

### Member App

- stable payment information section
- renew membership flow for frozen members
- reliable membership state sync
- clean QR payment workflow
- stable notifications and status messaging

### Supabase / Backend

- consistent event logging
- better audit history
- stronger background lifecycle automation
- billing and membership data integrity

## Phase 2: Better Than The Category

Goal: become more desirable than standard gym software.

### Dashboard

- smarter front desk with live alerts, failed payments, frozen members, and purchase activity
- retention workspace with actionable queues
- automation center for member nudges and staff follow-up
- owner recommendations and AI-assisted summaries

### Member App

- higher-polish training tab
- cleaner iOS-native interaction model
- challenge engine
- leaderboard engine
- better milestone surfaces
- deeper personal progress summaries

### Shared Experience

- gym news and announcements
- staff shoutouts and member spotlights
- in-gym product flows that feel native
- richer notification system tied to real gym behavior

## Phase 3: Hard To Copy

Goal: become the software that defines the gym experience, not just manages it.

### Culture OS

- PR board
- monthly challenges
- gym rankings
- streak competitions
- attendance campaigns
- custom gym rituals and recurring events

### Commerce OS

- merch drops
- fridge and snack purchases
- event tickets
- promos and offers
- member wallet with real repeat-use value

### Expansion

- multi-location controls
- branded gym-level themes and identity layers
- more advanced churn prevention
- optional scheduling or classes only if demanded by the business

## What We Build Next

This is the recommended execution order from here.

1. Memberships and billing dashboard
2. Front desk command center
3. Dashboard member detail view
4. Challenge and leaderboard engine
5. Member app visual polish pass
6. Reporting and audit pass
7. Culture OS layer

## Now / Next / Later

### Now

- finish memberships and billing admin
- make front desk the daily operator home screen
- make member lifecycle state reliable and visible everywhere
- make payment flows honest and resilient

### Next

- build challenges, leaderboards, and milestone systems
- improve training, PR tracking, and retention loops
- expand commerce features already seeded in the wallet flow

### Later

- advanced automations
- multi-location support
- richer CRM and sales tools
- optional class scheduling layer

## What Not To Overbuild Yet

- full class scheduling suite if the gym is not class-first
- complex CRM lead funnels before memberships and billing are stable
- enterprise multi-location controls before single-location operations feel excellent
- AI-heavy features that sit on top of unstable core workflows

## Success Metrics

We should judge roadmap success by:

- active members retained month over month
- member app weekly usage
- front desk workflow speed
- percent of members with clean billing state
- freeze-to-renew recovery rate
- gym news and challenge engagement
- merch and in-gym purchase adoption

## Competitive Goal

If we execute this correctly:

- Gymdesk should still look more generic than us
- Glofox should still look less gym-native than us
- our product should feel like the best software for a serious lifting gym, not the most generic fitness business
